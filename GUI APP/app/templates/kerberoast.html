About Kerberoasting

Kerberoasting is an Active Directory attack technique that targets service accounts with Service Principal Names (SPNs). Any authenticated domain user can request service tickets for these accounts — no administrative privileges are required, as this behavior is a normal feature of Microsoft's Kerberos implementation. It is not an exploit of a vulnerability, but rather an abuse of the design combined with weak or poorly managed service account passwords. The ticket is encrypted with the service account's password, allowing an attacker to extract it and attempt offline cracking. Because service account passwords are often old, weak, and not regularly rotated, they may be easier to recover. If compromised, these accounts may have elevated privileges or even local administrator rights on certain systems, enabling lateral or vertical movement within the network.

Recommendation

Use strong, long passwords for all service accounts and ensure they are rotated regularly, or preferably replace them with Group Managed Service Accounts (gMSAs) that automatically manage and rotate passwords.
